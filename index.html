All We Can Save

Why are we doing this?

A lot of us have climate change on our radar, but find it complicated to get involved or start learning.

It's a topic that can be intimidating and feel, these days, trendy. But, it's important!

There's something for everyone in talking about "the climate", and approaches abound: science, spiritualism, activism, art, urban planning, history, scuba diving, etc.

What

We're hoping to make a low-key event for learning about nature, climate change, and our local community. It won't be something that treats topics lightly, but it also won't be very formal. Just a space with the minimum structure for people to stay in touch.

It will be communal and reliable: a chance to chat about earth + eat crepes together every-other month.

That's it. If other things bloom out of this (activism, field trips, art projects) then cool! If not, at least we ate crepes.

What more

We'll use the book, All We Can Save, as a common touchstone to get started.

The book is an anthology of writings by 60 women at the forefront of the climate movement.

We encourage you to read the chapter "Community is our Best Chance", by Christine Nieves-Rodriguez, and/or listen to this podcast episode by our first meeting.

If you or anyone else needs a copy of the book, just ask! We have plenty.
